CLUB HISTORY

FOUNDATION & EARLY YEARS

Real Madrid was officially founded on March 6, 1902, as Madrid Football Club. The club was granted the title "Real" (Royal) by King Alfonso XIII in 1920. In its early years, the club dominated Spanish football, winning the first ever La Liga title in 1929.

EUROPEAN DOMINANCE

Real Madrid has won a record 15 UEFA Champions League titles, including the first five competitions from 1956 to 1960. The team of the 1950s, featuring Alfredo Di Stéfano, Ferenc Puskás, and Francisco Gento, is considered one of the greatest in football history.

DOMESTIC SUCCESS

The club has won 35 La Liga titles (most in Spain), 20 Copa del Rey trophies, and 12 Supercopa de España titles. Real Madrid holds the record for the most consecutive La Liga titles (5 from 1986-1990).

MODERN ERA

In 2000, FIFA named Real Madrid the "Club of the 20th Century". The Galácticos era (2000s) featured stars like Zidane, Ronaldo, and Beckham. Recent years have seen continued success with Cristiano Ronaldo leading the team to 4 UCL titles in 5 years (2014-2018).

SANTIAGO BERNABÉU STADIUM
STADIUM HISTORY

The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is a football stadium in Madrid, Spain. With a current seating capacity of 81,044, it has been the home stadium of Real Madrid since its completion in 1947.

The stadium is one of the world's most famous football venues. It has hosted the European Cup final four times: in 1957, 1969, 1980, and the 2010 UEFA Champions League Final. It was also a venue for the 1982 FIFA World Cup.

RENOVATION PROJECT

The stadium has undergone a massive €525 million renovation project that began in 2019 and was completed in 2023. The renovation includes:

  • New retractable roof covering the entire stadium
  • 360° video scoreboard
  • Improved accessibility features
  • Expanded hospitality areas
  • New exterior facade with interactive displays
  • Underground parking and commercial spaces
